Why Consulting Health Scoring is Different
In a SaaS business, health scoring is largely product-led — login frequency, feature adoption, and support ticket volume tell you most of what you need to know. In consulting and AI services, there is no product to log into. Health is relationship-driven and delivery-driven. A client can be highly satisfied and still at churn risk because their internal champion left, the project scope shifted, or a competitor made a move. The model below captures the four signals that actually matter in a consulting context: delivery quality, stakeholder engagement, commercial health, and strategic alignment.

RAG Tiers & Playbook Actions
🟢 Green
Score 75–100 · Healthy
Delivery on track, stakeholders engaged, renewal pipeline in place. No immediate risk. Primary focus is expansion and deepening the relationship.
  • Initiate expansion conversation — upsell, cross-sell, or AI extension
  • Explore reference or case study opportunity
  • Schedule QBR focused on next phase roadmap
  • Monthly CS check-in sufficient
🟡 Amber
Score 50–74 · At Risk
One or more signals degrading. Delivery slippage, declining stakeholder engagement, or no renewal conversation started. Needs active intervention before it slides to Red.
  • VP (Srini) + CS Lead joint call with executive sponsor within 2 weeks
  • Root cause analysis: which signal is driving the dip?
  • Recovery plan documented and shared with client
  • Weekly CS check-in; escalate to bi-weekly leadership touch
🔴 Red
Score 0–49 · Critical
Material churn or contract reduction risk. Could be delivery failure, executive sponsor departure, competitive threat, or budget cut. Requires immediate executive intervention.
  • Escalate immediately to VP (Srini) + SVP/GM
  • Executive-to-executive call within 1 week
  • Formal recovery plan with 30-day milestones
  • Flag in weekly forecast call — revenue at risk logged in CRM
