Preferred Tech Stack
Hi-Tech Business Vertical · Consulting & AI Services · Current + Recommended · FY2026
Stack Overview
The revenue tech stack for a consulting and AI services firm needs to do something product-led tools don't: track relationships, not logins. The current stack is intentionally lean — Salesforce as the system of record and ZoomInfo for data enrichment. The recommended additions fill the gaps in pipeline intelligence, CS operations, and AI-driven forecasting without adding unnecessary complexity.

Revenue Intelligence & Forecasting
Recommended
Gong
Conversation intelligence — records, transcribes, and analyses sales and CS calls. Surfaces deal risk, competitor mentions, and coaching opportunities.
In consulting, deals are won and lost in conversations. Gong makes those visible and coachable. Integrates with Salesforce.
Recommended
Clari
AI-driven revenue forecasting, pipeline inspection, and deal scoring. Gives the CRO a single, AI-weighted view of commit vs. best case vs. pipeline.
Replaces manual forecast rollup. Particularly valuable for a consulting model where deal timing is uncertain and stage probabilities are imprecise.
Customer Success & Health
Recommended
Gainsight
CS platform for health scoring, success plans, NPS collection, renewal tracking, and automated playbooks. Designed for relationship-led businesses.
Enables the health scoring model in this playbook. Tracks relationship signals and delivery quality, not just product usage.
Recommended
Totango
Lightweight alternative to Gainsight. Customer journey tracking, health scoring, and CS workflow automation. Lower implementation overhead.
Better fit if the CS team is small or early-stage. Faster to deploy than Gainsight with less configuration complexity.
AI & Automation Layer
AI Layer
6sense
Account-based intelligence platform — identifies in-market accounts using AI, predicts buying stage, and surfaces intent signals before prospects engage.
Particularly relevant given Hi-Tech vertical focus. Helps AEs prioritise outreach to accounts that are actively evaluating — before they raise their hand.
AI Layer
Salesforce Einstein
Native AI layer within Salesforce — opportunity scoring, lead scoring, activity capture, and predictive forecasting built into the existing CRM.
Zero integration cost. Activating Einstein on the existing Salesforce instance is the lowest-friction AI uplift available. Start here before adding external AI tools.
Stack Design Principles
Salesforce First
Every tool in the stack must integrate with Salesforce Sales Cloud. If it doesn't sync bidirectionally with the CRM, it creates a data silo. The CRO needs one version of truth — not five dashboards.
Relationship Over Product
In consulting, there's no product usage data. Every health and intelligence tool must be evaluated on whether it captures relationship signals — not logins, clicks, or feature adoption.
Activate Before Adding
The current stack has underutilised capabilities — Pardot's ABM features, Salesforce Einstein, ZoomInfo's intent signals. Fully activate what's already paid for before adding new tools to the stack.
AI at Every Layer
The AI services positioning needs to be backed by an AI-enabled revenue stack. Using intent AI (6sense), conversation AI (Gong), and forecasting AI (Clari/Einstein) makes the pitch credible — and keeps the team ahead of clients on AI adoption.
Rep Adoption First
A tool that AEs and CS Leads don't use is worse than no tool — it pollutes data quality. Every addition to the stack should be evaluated on adoption likelihood before ROI. Start with the fewest possible tools.
Measure Stack ROI
Each tool should have a defined metric it owns. Gong owns call quality score. Clari owns forecast accuracy. Gainsight owns health score freshness. If a tool can't be tied to a metric, reconsider the spend.
