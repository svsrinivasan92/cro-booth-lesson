Structure & Split Rationale
50/50 Split — The CRO Standard
A CRO designs comp to drive revenue growth, not account stability. The 50/50 split maximises performance incentive — half of earnings depend entirely on what the AE closes. This is the market norm for enterprise sales roles and signals that selling is the primary job, not managing existing accounts.

Quota Setting — Top-Down Logic
Quota is set top-down from the board revenue target. Board target ÷ headcount = per-AE quota, then over-allocated by 1.2× to account for ramp lag and attrition. The 10× OTE multiplier ensures the business generates sufficient margin to justify comp cost at quota attainment.

Attainment Metrics
New Logo Bookings
70%
Net-new accounts closed during the year. Primary CRO mandate — an AE who only manages existing accounts is not doing the job the CRO is paying for.
Expansion Bookings
30%
Net-new SOW value from existing accounts — upsells, cross-sells, scope increases. Must exceed prior-year value to count.
Renewal (Flat)
0%
Flat renewals are a CS and delivery responsibility — not a sales motion. Paying commission on flat renewals inflates cost without driving growth.
