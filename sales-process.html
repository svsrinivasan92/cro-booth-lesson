Exit Criteria Best Practices
Build into CRM
Each stage gate should be a required field or checkbox — not rep judgment. Probabilities should reflect historical close rates, not optimism. What gets measured in the system gets managed in the field.
Limit to 1–2 per stage
More than two exit criteria per stage and reps either game them or skip them during deal reviews. Keep each gate tight and unambiguous — one measurable buyer action, one measurable seller action.
Make them observable
"Prospect agreed" is not an exit criterion. "Verbal approval on record" or "signed NDA returned" is. If you can't point to an artifact or an action that happened, the stage hasn't been earned.
Split buyer vs. seller actions
A seller can complete their side and still be stuck waiting on the buyer. Track both. A deal where the seller has done everything but the buyer has gone quiet is not a Negotiating deal — it's a Developing deal.
Inspect every deal review
Exit criteria are only as useful as the discipline to enforce them in the forecast call. If a rep can't articulate the exit criterion for the current stage, the deal probability should be questioned immediately.
Review quarterly
Market conditions, deal cycles, and competitive dynamics shift. Exit criteria written 18 months ago may not reflect how deals actually move today. Quarterly reviews keep the process grounded in current reality, not historical assumptions.
